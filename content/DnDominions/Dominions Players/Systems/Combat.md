
# Fatigue
Penalties from fatigue depend on your Constitution score, which sets the threshold for when additional effects occur as multiples of this score. Fatigue Threshold 1 is any fatigue less than or equal to your constitution, threshold 2 is fatigue less than or equal to double your constitution, and so on. For example, a character with 11 Constitution having taken 11 fatigue suffers no penalties, while if they have 24 fatigue they experience Fatigue Threshold 3.

| Fatigue Threshold | Effects |
| ------------------- | -------- |
| 1 | No penalties |
| 2 | -1 AC |
| 3 |  -1 AC, -1 to hit |
| 4 | -2 AC, -1 to hit, -1 to all saves |
| 5 | -2 AC, -2 to hit, -1 to all saves, critical hits automatically confirm |
| 6 | -3 AC, -2 to hit, -1 to all saves, critical hits automatically confirm |
| 7 | -3 AC, -3 to hit, -1 to all saves, critical hits automatically confirm, cannot run or charge |
| 8 | -3 AC, -3 to hit, -2 to all saves, critical hits automatically confirm, cannot run or charge |
| 9| -3 AC, -3 to hit, -2 to all saves, critical hits automatically confirm, cannot run or charge, flat footed vs all attacks |
| 10 | unconscious |
| 11+ | unconscious, and each point of fatigue also deals 1 point of damage |

Fatigue naturally recovers at a rate of 1 per round. Poor morale or harsh conditions can also introduce **Permanent Fatigue**, essentially increasing the base fatigue which someone always suffers. This fatigue can only be recovered once the conditions causing it are alleviated.

## Encumbrance
Creatures in combat gain fatigue equal to their encumbrance each round, a value which typically depends on armor but can also be influenced by the environment.
